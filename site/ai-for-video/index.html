Overview

What Is AI for Video?

AI for video encompasses tools that use machine learning to create, edit, and enhance video content. The field has exploded with capabilities that seemed impossible just two years ago - from generating photorealistic video from text descriptions to creating talking avatar videos without any filming.

The AI video landscape breaks into four main categories:

🎬

Video Generation

Create video clips from text prompts or images. Runway, Sora, Pika.

✂

AI-Powered Editing

Edit video using AI features like text-based editing. Descript, CapCut.

👤

AI Avatars

Generate talking head videos with AI presenters. HeyGen, Synthesia.

🔁

Repurposing Tools

Turn long videos into shorts and clips. Opus Clip, Vidyo.

💡

The 2026 AI Video Landscape

Text-to-video has reached commercial viability with tools like Runway Gen-3 and Sora. AI avatars are now indistinguishable from real footage for many use cases. The biggest shift: AI video tools are becoming accessible to solo creators, not just studios with big budgets.

The Modern Creator Stack

Most successful video creators use multiple tools. A typical stack: Runway or Sora for B-roll generation, Descript for editing talking head content, CapCut for short-form finishing, and Opus Clip for repurposing. Start with one tool and expand as needed.

Trends

AI Video Trends in 2026

Text-to-Video Goes Mainstream

What seemed like sci-fi in 2023 is now practical. Runway Gen-3 and Sora produce clips good enough for commercial use. Creators are using AI-generated footage as B-roll, for music videos, and even for entire short films. The quality gap between AI and traditional footage shrinks monthly.

Avatar Quality Reaches Uncanny Valley

HeyGen and Synthesia avatars have become nearly indistinguishable from real footage. Companies now routinely use AI presenters for training, product demos, and even customer-facing content. The cost savings compared to traditional video production are dramatic.

Multilingual Content at Scale

AI dubbing and lip-sync have made it practical to produce content in 20+ languages from a single recording. HeyGen's video translation and ElevenLabs' dubbing let creators reach global audiences without reshooting anything.

Editing Becomes Conversational

The future of editing is describing what you want. Tools increasingly let you say "remove all the ums" or "make this section more energetic" and have AI handle the cuts and effects. Descript pioneered this; others are following.
